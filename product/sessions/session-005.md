## Narrative Summary

This was the first session to run the full content pipeline. Phase 3 was confirmed ready to begin in parallel with continued Phase 2 asset sourcing — the asset library had enough approved backgrounds across PDH and DFC categories to support production.

The session ran Stages 1 through 5 sequentially: Researcher (Content Opportunity Brief), SEO Agent (Discoverability Brief), Content Writer (Content Brief), Designer (Asset Delivery Note), and Asset Manager (verification and staging). All five stages completed for content piece 001 — "You Are Not Behind."

The content theme selected by the Researcher was an identity-shift reframe piece targeting the audience's quiet experience of feeling behind in life. The Researcher identified this as the strongest possible first piece: it activates the engagement mechanics most valuable at zero-follower launch (saves and DM shares), establishes the account's differentiated mentor voice immediately, and is evergreen. Three angles were presented; the Writer selected Angle 1 — "You are not behind. You are becoming."

The Content Brief went through an SD review that surfaced two important tone standards that have now been captured in memory for future briefs. First: the account must never place the audience's limitations on external sources ("everyone told you," "you were handed"). The obstacle is always internal — the audience authored their own story, and that means they can rewrite it. Second: overlay text must be immediately parseable in isolation — pronouns must have clear referents within the same slide, and figurative phrases that need explanation are not ready. A third learning — slide copy that reads cleanly in the brief but contains an ambiguous metaphor (Slide 2 "quietly ahead of your own life") should be caught at the writing stage, not the review stage.

The Designer specified the full visual production brief: `bg-pdh-001-sway-slow.mp4` as a continuous loop across all six slides, the complete typography and placement spec for each slide, a single gold accent on the word "built" in Slide 5, and full animation and export instructions. The Asset Manager verified the Delivery Note against the Content Brief — all overlay text confirmed exact — and registered the two expected files as Pending Production in the asset status log.

The pipeline is now paused at Stage 5, waiting for the operator to produce the physical assets using the visual spec. Once delivered, Stage 6 (Publisher) proceeds.

One process note from this session: pipeline stages ran in the foreground, blocking the conversation thread. Going forward, pipeline stages should run in the background — the stages are still sequential (each waits for the prior stage's output) but the conversation remains responsive during execution.

## For the Product

- **The content pipeline runs cleanly as a sequential agent chain.** Stages 1–5 completed without cross-agent confusion. Each agent read the prior stage's outputs and produced the next handoff document without additional instruction. The pipeline design (each stage has defined inputs, outputs, and a clear scope boundary) is what makes this work — agents don't need to coordinate, they just read and write.
- **SD review at the Content Brief stage is load-bearing.** The two tone standards surfaced in this review (no external blame; overlay text parseable in isolation) were not explicitly documented anywhere before this session. They emerged only from seeing the draft. This is why the SD approval gate exists — not to approve what the agents wrote, but to catch the things the agents don't yet know to avoid. The memory system then closes the loop so the agents know next time.
- **"Internal vs. external" is the most important tone distinction for this account.** Content that places blame or limitation on external sources (other people, society, the past) feels like a victim narrative and undermines the core message. The audience needs to feel that they have full agency — and that requires locating every obstacle inside the self. This is a brand principle, not just a style preference.
- **The cover frame is a separate production step, not a video export.** It must be produced as a still JPEG independently of the video reel. First-time operators often assume the cover frame can be extracted from the video — it cannot reliably be done without quality loss and composition compromise. The cover is a separate production file.
- **Run pipeline agents in the background.** Stages are still sequential (each waits for prior output), but running in background keeps the conversation thread responsive so the operator can continue working or asking questions during execution.
