## Critical Platform Context — Read Before Using Any Set

Instagram formalized a hard limit of three to five hashtags per post or Reel in early 2026. This fundamentally changes how hashtags function for this account.

**What this means in practice:**
- The old logic of 20-30 mixed hashtags is obsolete. Do not apply it.
- Each hashtag slot must carry genuine strategic weight. Filler tags consume slots that could be working.
- Hashtags now function primarily as content-filing labels for Instagram's AI — they confirm the category of a post to the algorithm. They are not a direct driver of discovery through hashtag browsing.
- Caption SEO (keyword-rich caption text) now carries more discoverability weight than hashtags. Hashtags are supplementary context, not the engine. See `/seo/discoverability-framework.md` for caption SEO rules.
- Posts with zero hashtags can outperform hashtag-heavy posts in some conditions. Hashtag presence alone does not guarantee reach.

**The selection logic for this account:** With three to five slots, each post should use one broad category-confirmation tag, one to two mid-tier niche tags that signal the specific content category, and one to two micro or low-competition tags that align with the account's differentiated positioning. This is not a rigid formula — adjust based on the specific content and which tier tags are most relevant. Never use five hashtags if three strong ones cover the content better.

## Tier Definitions

| Tier | Volume Range | Function |
|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 — Broad | 5M+ posts | Category confirmation for the algorithm. Low discovery value for new accounts but signals content type. Use one per post maximum. |
| Tier 2 — Mid-Niche | 500K–5M posts | The strategic core. Specific enough to surface, large enough to carry reach potential. Use one to two per post. |
| Tier 3 — Micro | Under 500K posts | Lowest competition. Attracts small but highly engaged, high-intent audiences. Differentiating. Use one to two per post. |

## Rotation Strategy and Shadowban Risk

**Why rotation matters:** Repeatedly using identical hashtag sets across consecutive posts can trigger Instagram's spam-detection systems, reducing distribution. This is commonly referred to as shadowbanning, though Instagram does not officially confirm the mechanism.

**Rotation rules for this account:**

1. Do not use the same hashtag set on consecutive posts. Always rotate to a different set, even within the same pillar.
2. Allow a minimum of three to four posts before repeating any individual hashtag, where possible.
3. Treat the micro-tier tags (#identityshift, #gratitudepractice, #scarcitymindset, #timeiswealth) as rotational assets — they are the differentiating tags and their value depends on not being overused.
4. Tier 1 tags (#personalgrowth, #gratitude, #mindset, #manifestation) can appear more frequently because they function as category labels, not discovery drivers. Even so, vary which one appears per post.
5. When the Analyst flags a hashtag as underperforming, retire it from active rotation immediately and test a replacement from this document's Tier 3 options.

**Monthly hashtag audit:** At the monthly review, compare the rotation log (maintained by Publishing) against Analyst performance data. Retire any tag showing consistently low reach contribution. Add replacements from the Tier 3 pools or from new micro-tags identified during the review cycle.
