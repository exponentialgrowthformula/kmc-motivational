## How to Use This Document

This library organizes keywords and phrases by content pillar. Each entry records:

- **Search intent** — what the user is seeking when they use this term on Instagram search.
- **Competition** — estimated difficulty for a new account to surface: High / Mid / Low. Based on observed post volume and niche saturation from raw hashtag observations.
- **Recommended use** — where this keyword should be deployed:
  - **Caption** — work naturally into the caption body. Instagram's search algorithm reads caption text and weights keyword-rich descriptions. This is the primary discoverability mechanism in 2026.
  - **Overlay** — suitable for on-screen text. Instagram's OCR reads text in images and video frames, reinforcing the content category signal to the algorithm.
  - **Hashtag** — this exact term or its hashtag variant is a candidate for one of the three-to-five hashtag slots. See `/seo/hashtag-sets.md` for curated, ready-to-use sets.

**Platform context for 2026:** Instagram now hard-limits posts to three to five hashtags. Caption SEO carries the primary discoverability load. Every caption should use at least two to three primary keywords naturally in the body text. Overlay text reinforces the category signal but does not substitute for caption keywords.

## Keyword Maintenance Protocol

- **Monthly review:** Cross-reference this library against the most recent Analyst Insight Brief before issuing new Discoverability Briefs. Retire any keyword the Analyst flags as underperforming or algorithmically misaligned.
- **Retirement rule:** Do not retire a keyword without identifying a direct replacement from audience language, search bar observation, or competitor caption analysis. Keyword gaps reduce caption SEO strength.
- **Addition protocol:** New keywords should be observed in the wild before being added — competitor account captions, Instagram search bar autocomplete suggestions, and comment language are the best sources.
- **Seasonal flags:** Note any keyword that shows strong seasonal or event-driven search spikes by adding a note in the relevant pillar section at review time.
