## 1. The 2026 Instagram Discoverability Model

### What changed

Instagram's discoverability model has shifted materially. The following principles govern how this account's content is found:

**Caption SEO is now the primary discoverability signal.** Instagram's search algorithm reads caption text and weights keyword-rich descriptions in a manner closer to Google-style keyword relevance than it did in earlier years. A caption that uses niche-specific language naturally increases findability in Instagram search independently of hashtag presence.

**Hashtags are category-confirmation labels, not discovery drivers.** The hard three-to-five hashtag limit made precision mandatory. Hashtags now tell the algorithm what category to file the content under — they are not a mechanism for reaching large audiences through hashtag browsing. A post with three precise hashtags will outperform a post with five generic ones.

**Engagement signals drive algorithmic distribution.** The algorithm's top three distribution signals for Reels are: watch time and completion rate; DM shares (sends per reach is confirmed by Instagram leadership as the strongest signal for reaching non-followers); and likes-per-reach ratio. Discoverability strategy must support these signals — a well-optimized caption and hook increases both completion rate and share behavior.

**On-screen text (overlay) is read by Instagram's OCR system.** Text visible in images and video frames is extracted and used as an additional category signal. Overlay text reinforces the caption keywords. This is a secondary signal, not a substitute for caption SEO.

**Zero-hashtag posts can outperform hashtag-heavy posts.** This is not a recommendation to abandon hashtags — it confirms that hashtag presence alone is not a quality signal. Content quality and engagement patterns are the underlying drivers. Hashtags supplement; they do not compensate for weak content.

## 2. Caption Structure for Discoverability

### The caption's dual job

Every caption on this account has two jobs simultaneously:

1. **Human job:** Hook the reader, deliver resonant value, prompt engagement (save, share, comment, follow).
2. **Algorithm job:** Use niche-specific keywords in natural language so Instagram's search system can correctly file and surface the content.

These jobs are not in conflict. Keyword-rich language and compelling human language can coexist in the same sentence. The discipline is writing for both simultaneously rather than treating SEO as a layer added after the human copy is written.

### Caption architecture

The following structure applies to all Reels and carousel posts on this account.

**Line 1 — The Hook (1–2 sentences):**
The first line must do two things before the "more" fold: stop the scroll and deploy the primary keyword or phrase. The hook should read as a compelling human statement, not an SEO label. The keyword should sit inside a sentence the audience would want to read regardless of whether they were looking for it.

Example of weak hook (keyword forced): "Personal growth content about identity shift today."
Example of strong hook (keyword embedded naturally): "The identity shift you're waiting for isn't coming. It starts with the decision you keep postponing."

Instagram search weights the first line of the caption heavily. The primary keyword for the post belongs here.

**Lines 2–4 — The Body (2–4 sentences, optional but recommended for Reels):**
Extend the hook's idea. This is where one to two secondary keywords can be introduced naturally. Keep sentences short — this is a mobile-first environment. The body does not need to summarize the carousel; it should complement it or deepen the entry point.

**Final Line — The CTA (1 sentence):**
A save or share prompt. Keep it direct and specific. "Save this when you need a reminder" outperforms generic "double tap if you agree" prompts because it explicitly signals the behavior the algorithm rewards (saves). The CTA is not an SEO placement but it drives the engagement signal that compounds discoverability over time.

**Line breaks:** Use single line breaks between sections. Walls of text reduce caption reads on mobile. White space is functional, not decorative.

### Caption length

For Reels: 100–150 words is the target range. Long enough to carry keyword depth; short enough to read in the time it takes to watch the Reel. Captions longer than 200 words are rarely read in full on mobile and dilute the keyword density.

For carousels and static posts where caption reading is more likely: up to 200 words is acceptable.

### Keyword density and placement rules

- Place the primary keyword in the first line of the caption.
- Place one to two secondary keywords in the body naturally.
- Do not force keywords. A keyword used unnaturally is worse than a keyword omitted — it reduces readability, which reduces completion rate, which reduces algorithmic distribution.
- Use the exact phrase from the keyword library where possible. "Personal growth journey" carries more search weight than "journey toward growth" because the library phrase reflects actual search behavior.
- Do not repeat the same keyword more than once in a caption. Repetition signals keyword stuffing to the algorithm and reads poorly to humans.

## 3. Hook Optimization for Search and Completion Rate

### Why the hook is the SEO and engagement linchpin

The first line of the caption and the first frame of the Reel are the two most important discoverability assets in every post. Both are read by the algorithm and both determine whether a human engages past the opening.

For Reels specifically, the first three seconds of the video determine whether the algorithm classifies the content as high-quality (high watch time, likely to be pushed to non-followers) or low-quality (early drop-off, reduced distribution). The first slide of a Carousel Reel functions the same way.

### Hook types aligned with this account's content patterns

The following hook structures are observed to perform well in this niche and are aligned with this account's voice. All can embed primary keywords naturally.

**The myth-bust hook:** Challenges a commonly held belief. Opens a gap in the reader's assumptions. "Manifestation isn't magic. It's what happens when who you are aligns with what you want." — primary keyword: manifestation; secondary: who you are aligns.

**The quiet-naming hook:** Names the audience's quiet, undramatic experience without dramatizing it. "You've been careful for so long that careful stopped feeling like a choice." — no keyword required; this hook earns engagement through emotional recognition. Follow with a keyword in line two.

**The identity-challenge hook:** Challenges how the audience sees themselves rather than what they should do. "You are not behind. You are becoming." — no hard keyword but identity language reinforces the category signal.

**The reframe hook:** Takes a common concept and turns it. "Gratitude isn't about what you have. It's about what you're training your brain to see." — primary keyword: gratitude; embedded naturally in a reframe.

**The cost-naming hook:** Names what something costs — playing it safe, staying quiet, waiting. "Playing it safe has a price. Most people just pay it slowly enough not to notice." — primary phrase: playing it safe.

**Note for the Content Writer:** The SEO Agent provides primary and secondary keywords in the Discoverability Brief. The Writer's job is to embed them inside one of these hook structures (or a structural variant) — not to stack keywords at the front of the caption as labels.

## 4. Overlay Text (On-Screen Text) SEO

### How Instagram reads overlay text

Instagram's OCR system extracts visible text from image and video frames. This text is treated as an additional category signal alongside the caption and hashtags. It reinforces the algorithm's classification of the content.

### Overlay text strategy for this account

This account's content is text-forward by design — the entire message is delivered through on-screen text overlays. This is a structural SEO advantage. Every post naturally reinforces its keyword category through the overlay text on every slide.

**Overlay text rules for maximum SEO value:**

- Use niche-specific language in the overlay, not generic motivational language. "Your identity creates your reality" carries more category signal than "Believe in yourself."
- The first slide's overlay text should include or echo the caption's primary keyword. The algorithm reads Reel thumbnails and first frames — the first slide is the highest-signal overlay text position.
- Do not use overlay text that contradicts the caption keywords. Algorithmic confusion about content category reduces distribution.
- Use complete, readable sentences on overlays. Truncated phrases or cryptic single words are less useful as category signals than natural phrases that match how people search.
- Text size and legibility are not SEO decisions but they affect watch time. Text that cannot be read on mobile without zooming creates drop-off, which reduces completion rate, which reduces distribution. Legibility is a discoverability factor by proxy.

### Keywords suitable for overlay use

The keyword library (`/seo/keyword-library.md`) flags keywords as "Overlay" where the phrase is natural as spoken-word or displayed text. These are the priority overlay terms. Keywords flagged as "Caption only" are search terms that work in text but can read awkwardly as displayed visual statements — keep them in the caption.

## 5. Hashtag Placement and Configuration

### Caption body vs. first comment

Current evidence and Instagram's own guidance do not strongly differentiate between hashtags placed in the caption body and hashtags placed in the first comment for reach performance. The SEO Agent's recommendation for this account is to place hashtags at the end of the caption body, below the CTA, separated by a line break.

Rationale: First-comment hashtags require an additional step at publishing and are unnecessary complexity given the three-to-five tag limit. When hashtags were 20-30 tags long, moving them to the first comment cleaned up the visual presentation. With three to five tags, they are visually unobtrusive in the caption.

**Standard placement format:**
```
[Caption body]

[CTA line]

#hashtag1 #hashtag2 #hashtag3
```

### Hashtag selection per post

Refer to `/seo/hashtag-sets.md` for pillar-specific curated sets. The selection rule:

- One Tier 1 tag (category confirmation)
- One to two Tier 2 tags (niche targeting)
- One to two Tier 3 tags (micro, differentiated)
- Total: three to five tags

Never fill slots with oversaturated or irrelevant tags to reach the five-tag maximum. Three strong tags outperform five weak ones.

### Hashtag rotation

Rotate sets across posts as defined in the hashtag sets document. Do not use the same hashtag set on consecutive posts. The Publishing Agent maintains the rotation log; the SEO Agent reviews it monthly against Analyst performance data.

## 6. Platform-Specific Optimization — Instagram Reels

### Carousel Reel format advantages

The primary format for this account is Carousel Reels — video with carousel-style frame transitions set to music. This format combines:

- **Engagement mechanics of carousels:** Swipe-through time counts toward engagement duration. Saves are common because carousels reward saving for re-reading. Both signals are weighted by the algorithm.
- **Discoverability mechanics of Reels:** 55% of Reel views currently come from non-followers. Reels are the strongest format for reaching the target audience before any follower base exists.

### Audio selection and SEO

Trending audio creates an association signal between the track and a content category. For a new account, using rising audio — before it peaks — gives an algorithmic advantage because the platform pushes tracks with momentum.

The audio itself is not a keyword SEO tool. But audio selection affects the audience segment the algorithm pushes content toward. Calming instrumental tracks (identified in the trend report as a strong match for this account's aesthetic) attract the mindfulness, personal growth, and gratitude-adjacent audience segments on Instagram's recommendation graph.

Audio timing recommendations are routed to the Publisher. The SEO Agent notes audio fit in Discoverability Briefs where relevant.

### Alt text

Instagram allows custom alt text for all posts. Alt text is read by the algorithm and by screen readers. Every post should have a custom alt text description.

**Alt text formula:** "[Content category] quote: [primary keyword phrase embedded in a brief description of the visual]. For people working on [pillar theme]."

Example: "Personal growth quote: identity shift — white text over a dark mountain landscape. For people working on becoming who they know they can be."

The alt text should be written in the Discoverability Brief by the SEO Agent and applied by the Publishing Agent at posting. It should not repeat the caption verbatim — a distinct natural-language description carries more signal value than a duplicate.

### Instagram search bar behavior

Instagram search now operates on keyword-first logic for content discovery, not just account discovery. Users typing "gratitude practice" or "abundance mindset" into the search bar will surface posts with those terms in the caption. This is a growing discoverability channel distinct from hashtag browsing and algorithmic Reels feed.

Search-optimized captions compound over time — a well-keyworded post from week one can continue generating search traffic for months. This is the long-tail discoverability argument for consistent caption SEO discipline from day one.

## 7. Seasonal and Trend-Responsive Discoverability

### When trending topics create search spikes

Certain events, seasons, and cultural moments generate temporary search spikes for specific terms. The Researcher's weekly trend report will flag these when relevant. When a trending topic aligns with a content pillar, the SEO Agent updates the Discoverability Brief to include the trend-aligned keyword.

**Rules for trend-responsive keywords:**
- Only incorporate trending keywords where they fit naturally and align with the account's pillars and voice. Do not chase trends that require tone or topic compromise.
- Note the trend's estimated peak window in the Discoverability Brief. Trending keywords become diluted quickly — timing matters.
- Do not retire evergreen keywords to accommodate trend terms. Add the trend term as a supplementary caption phrase; maintain the evergreen keyword.

### Seasonal search patterns to track

These are recurring seasonal windows where specific keywords in this niche spike predictably. Flag these in Discoverability Briefs when the calendar approaches them.

| Window | Keywords That Spike | Relevant Pillars |
|---|---|---|
| New Year (Dec 25 – Jan 15) | personal growth, self-improvement, habit change, goals, manifestation | Personal Growth, Manifestation |
| New Month (1st–3rd of each month) | gratitude practice, daily mindset, intentions, new month affirmations | Gratitude, Manifestation |
| Spring (March–April) | fresh start, personal transformation, letting go, becoming better | Personal Growth, Confidence |
| Back-to-School / Fall Reset (Aug–Sept) | discipline, mindset shift, goals, self-improvement | Personal Growth, Confidence |
| End of year (Oct–Nov) | abundance mindset, gratitude, year in review, reflection | Gratitude, Abundance Mindset |

## 8. Discoverability Brief Protocol

### What triggers a Discoverability Brief

A Discoverability Brief is produced for every content piece entering the pipeline. It is not produced retroactively — it must accompany the content into the writing stage.

### What the brief contains

Each brief uses the standard template from the SEO Agent instruction file and includes:

- Platform targets and format
- Primary keywords (two to four, drawn from the keyword library)
- Ready-to-use hashtag set (drawn from the hashtag sets document, with the specific set ID noted)
- Placement notes (hashtag position, any post-specific configuration)
- Alt text suggestion
- Notes on trending search terms or seasonal considerations

### Delivery

The Discoverability Brief travels with the content through the pipeline. It is delivered to the Content Writer alongside the Content Opportunity Brief. It is referenced by the Publishing Agent at posting. It is not an optional component — content without a Discoverability Brief should not proceed to the writing stage.

## 9. Discoverability Audit Process

### Monthly audit scope

At each monthly review, the SEO Agent produces a Discoverability Audit Report covering:

1. **Hashtag performance review:** Which hashtag sets correlated with above-average reach in the Analyst's data. Which sets correlated with below-average reach. Retirement and replacement decisions.
2. **Caption keyword performance:** Which keyword-forward captions are generating search-driven traffic (identified through Instagram Insights search discovery metric). Which pillars are generating search traffic and which are not.
3. **Alt text coverage:** Confirm all published posts have custom alt text. Flag any gaps to Publishing.
4. **Keyword library updates:** New terms to add, terms to retire, emerging audience language identified through comments and search.
5. **Hashtag set updates:** Retire underperforming tags, promote watch-list tags that have shown positive signals, update rotation sets.

The audit report is delivered to the Analyst for integration into the performance reporting framework.

## 10. Constraints and Boundaries

The following apply to all discoverability work on this account:

- **No religious or spiritual language in keywords, captions, or hashtags.** See the full banned list in `/seo/hashtag-sets.md`. This constraint is not negotiable and applies to every deliverable the SEO Agent produces.
- **No quick-fix keyword framing.** Phrases like "X days to change your life," "instant results," or "one trick" are not used in this account's discoverability strategy.
- **No hustle-culture keyword alignment.** Tags and keywords that associate this account with hustle-culture, bro-culture, or get-rich-quick content are avoided. Monitor algorithm association when using business-growth keywords — pair them with autonomy language, not achievement or grinding language.
- **No keyword stuffing.** Keywords used unnaturally reduce readability, reduce completion rate, and reduce algorithmic distribution. The discipline is natural placement, not maximum density.
- **Cross-platform expansion is not in scope.** This account is Instagram-only at launch. Cross-platform discoverability frameworks for TikTok, Pinterest, and YouTube Shorts will be built if and when the Strategy Director approves expansion after the first 90-day review.
