### Rationale

The three tags in P-6 cover adjacent but distinct conceptual territory from P-3, without duplicating any of its terms.

`#innerwork` (Tier 2, Confidence/Self-Worth and Personal Growth cross-pillar) names the mechanism at the center of Format C's arc — the recognition that the story the audience has been carrying is one they wrote, and therefore one they can rewrite. This is interior, identity-level work, which is precisely what "innerwork" signals to both the algorithm and high-intent searchers. It appears in Set X-6 alongside the identity-shift cluster, confirming its algorithm-category association with content like this.

`#selfgrowth` (Tier 2, Personal Growth) provides the growth-category signal that P-3 achieved through `#personalgrowthjourney`, but without repeating that tag. It is moderately less saturated than `#personalgrowth` itself, giving a zero-follower account a better surface-ability ceiling. It files the post correctly as personal growth content while the other two tags do the differentiating work.

`#becomingbetter` (Tier 3, Personal Growth; lower volume) maps directly to Line 7 of the Format C stacked reveal — "You are not behind. You are becoming." — which is the payoff line of the entire piece. The word "becoming" is the resolution the content builds toward across all seven lines. Using `#becomingbetter` as the micro-tier tag creates semantic coherence between the on-screen text, the search signal, and the audience state the post is targeting: someone in forward motion, not someone who has arrived. It is low-competition, which is appropriate for a zero-follower account seeking to rank within a reachable pool.

Together the set covers the internal transformation mechanism (inner work), the growth category (self-growth), and the identity-in-progress framing that is the core emotional register of Format C (becoming better). P-3 led with the identity-shift label and the journey framing; P-6 leads with the interior work and the active-becoming arc — adjacent territory, different vocabulary, no tag overlap.
